Uncontrolled Substance

1999
 🔈💻 Owned Loud
Deck's long-awaited solo debut, arriving after years of devastating guest verses across the Wu-Tang catalog. Produced primarily by RZA, True Master, 4th Disciple, and Deck himself, it showcased his gift for stacking dense multisyllabic rhymes over dark, sample-heavy production. The album balanced gritty street narratives with lyrical showcases, establishing Deck as one of the most technically skilled writers in hip-hop even outside the Wu collective.

The Movement

2003
Koch
Deck's sophomore effort, released independently through Koch Records after parting ways with Loud. Produced primarily by Ayatollah and Phantom of the Beats, the album pursued a grittier, more street-oriented sound with Deck's intricate lyricism riding harder, more aggressive production. Guest spots from Kool G Rap, Street Life, and Killa Sin reinforced the album's uncompromising street-rap identity.

The Resident Patient

2006
Urban Icon
Named after a Sherlock Holmes story, Deck's third album arrived on his own Urban Icon imprint with production from Mondee, Cilvaringz, Psycho Les of The Beatnuts, and Deck himself. The record balanced rugged Housegang posse cuts with more personal, reflective material, and featured appearances from Masta Killa, U-God, and the Housegang crew. It showcased a more independent Deck, steering his own creative direction outside any major label framework.

Manifesto

2010
Urban Icon
Deck's fourth solo album enlisted a deep bench of producers including The Alchemist, MoSS, Lee Bannon, and Agallah, alongside an impressive guest roster featuring Raekwon, Cormega, Termanology, Planet Asia, Cappadonna, Kurupt, and M.O.P. The production leaned into hard-hitting, sample-driven boom bap, and the album represented a statement of creative independence with Deck serving as his own executive producer on Urban Icon Records.

Chamber No. 9

2019
Urban Icon
After a nine-year gap, Deck returned with a concise, 13-track statement that reaffirmed his position within the Wu-Tang legacy. The emotionally charged tribute track to the late Ol' Dirty Bastard stood as one of the most personal moments in his catalog, while the rest of the record blended classic boom-bap textures with modern production from Danny Caiazzo and Boo Bundy. Features from Cappadonna, Streetlife, Trife Diesel, and Hellfire kept it firmly in the Wu-Tang family orbit.

CZARFACE

2013
Brick
The debut from the hip-hop supergroup uniting Inspectah Deck with underground duo 7L & Esoteric, built around a comic-book superhero concept with 7L's heavy, sample-driven production. Guest appearances from Action Bronson, Ghostface Killah, Roc Marciano, and Cappadonna added firepower to a record that revitalized Deck's creative output and launched one of the most prolific collaborations of the 2010s.

Every Hero Needs a Villain

2015
Brick
The sophomore CZARFACE album deepened the comic-book mythology while expanding the guest roster to include Method Man, GZA, Large Professor, MF DOOM, and RA The Rugged Man. 7L's production grew more cinematic and sample-heavy, and the chemistry between Deck and Esoteric sharpened considerably, yielding what many consider the group's strongest front-to-back listen.

Silver Age

Inspired by spaghetti westerns and grindhouse cinema, the record delivered a focused, self-contained statement of the group's artistic identity.

Czarface Meets Metal Face

Silver Age Get On Down
A full-length collaboration with the late MF DOOM that married CZARFACE's comic-book superhero aesthetic with DOOM's supervillain persona. The album's playful, referential lyricism and dense boom-bap production created a natural meeting point between two of underground hip-hop's most conceptually inventive acts, with additional appearances from Open Mike Eagle and Vinnie Paz.

Czarface Meets Ghostface

2019
Silver Age
A Wu-Tang family reunion of sorts, pairing Deck with his Clan brother Ghostface Killah for a full-length collaboration. The two MCs traded verses with the effortless chemistry of artists who had been rhyming together for decades, with 7L and Esoteric providing the connective tissue. The record balanced nostalgia with forward momentum, proving that Wu-Tang's creative well was far from dry.

Super What?

2021
Silver Age
The second and final CZARFACE collaboration with MF DOOM, completed before DOOM's passing in October 2020 and released posthumously. The album served as both a creative triumph and an unintentional farewell, with DOOM delivering some of his most playful and technically dazzling performances. A poignant document of an artist at the height of his powers.
