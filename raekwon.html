From the purple-tape blueprint of Cuban Linx through two decades of solo work, he built one of rap's most vivid and sustained creative visions.

Only Built 4 Cuban Linx…

1995
 🔈💻 Owned Loud
The purple tape — a mafioso-rap landmark structured as a crime film, with Raekwon starring as Lex Diamond and Ghostface Killah co-starring as Tony Starks. RZA's production wove kung-fu samples, soul loops, and gritty textures into a claustrophobic street-opera sound that became the template for narrative hip hop. The album introduced the Wu-Gambino aliases and elevated the storytelling ambitions of the entire Wu-Tang collective.

Immobilarity

1999
Loud
A deliberate shift away from the Cuban Linx formula, trading RZA's grimy minimalism for a broader palette of producers and a more reflective tone. The album found Raekwon navigating post-fame disillusionment and street politics without Ghostface as constant foil. Critics were divided, but tracks like "Yae Yo" and the Pete Rock–produced "All I Got Is You Pt. 2" demonstrated that the Chef's narrative instincts remained sharp even in new sonic territory.

The Lex Diamond Story

2003
 💻 In library Ice H2O Universal
A return to the crime-narrative concept under Raekwon's Lex Diamond alias, now on his own Ice H2O imprint. RZA returned alongside new producers Emile Haynie and DJ Khalil, and the Wu-Tang family showed up in force — Ghostface, Method Man, Masta Killa, Inspectah Deck, and Cappadonna all contributed. Guest turns from Fat Joe and Havoc broadened the album's reach beyond the Wu universe.

Only Built 4 Cuban Linx… Pt. II

2009
 💻 In library Ice H2O EMI
The long-awaited sequel, a decade in the making, that reunited Raekwon with RZA while recruiting an all-star production roster — J Dilla, Dr. Dre, Pete Rock, The Alchemist, Erick Sermon, and Marley Marl all contributed beats. Ghostface returned as co-star, and the album's sprawling 22 tracks delivered the cinematic street narratives fans had been waiting for. Widely regarded as one of the finest hip-hop sequels ever recorded.

Shaolin vs. Wu-Tang

2011
 💻 In library Ice H2O EMI
Named after the 1983 Shaw Brothers kung-fu film, this fifth solo album channeled the martial-arts mythology that had always infused Wu-Tang's aesthetic. A deep bench of producers — Scram Jones, The Alchemist, Erick Sermon, Bronze Nazareth, DJ Khalil, Mathematics — delivered beats that balanced boom-bap grit with cinematic sweep. Guest spots from Nas, Black Thought, Busta Rhymes, and the Wu faithful gave it a summit-meeting quality.

Fly International Luxurious Art

2015
Ice H2O Caroline
Raekwon's most outward-looking record, enlisting mainstream collaborators — A$AP Rocky, French Montana, 2 Chainz, Snoop Dogg, Rick Ross — alongside Ghostface and a globe-trotting production palette from Jerry Wonda, Swizz Beatz, and S1. The acronym F.I.L.A. signaled an ambition to move beyond the Wu-Tang orbit, blending luxury-rap polish with the Chef's street storytelling DNA.

The Wild

2017
 💻 In library Ice H2O Empire
Raekwon's seventh studio album cast a wider net than ever, featuring Lil Wayne, CeeLo Green, G-Eazy, and Andra Day alongside an eclectic production team including Dame Grease, J.U.S.T.I.C.E. League, and RoadsArt. The record moved between introspective street narratives and contemporary trap-inflected production, reflecting two decades of evolution from the purple-tape era while maintaining the Chef's unmistakable storytelling voice.
