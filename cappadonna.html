Cappadonna

Born Darryl Hill, Wu-Tang Clan's unofficial tenth member — later made official on 8 Diagrams — whose dense, slang-heavy rhyme style and flamboyant delivery became a signature element of the Clan's late-nineties peak, from his scene-stealing verse on Raekwon's "Ice Cream" through the Wu-Tang Forever sessions and a prolific solo career spanning nearly two decades.

The Pillage

1998
 💻 In library Razor Sharp Epic
Cappadonna's solo debut arrived at the absolute zenith of the Wu-Tang empire, debuting at number three on the Billboard 200 and topping the R&B chart with 132,000 first-week copies. Executive produced by the RZA with Ghostface Killah, the album channels the grimy, sample-heavy Wu production aesthetic through True Master, Goldfinghaz, Mathematics, and 4th Disciple. Dense with Clan cameos and the baroque street lyricism that defined the era, it earned Gold certification and remains his most celebrated work.

The Yin and the Yang

2001
Razor Sharp Epic
The sophomore release retains the Razor Sharp/Epic infrastructure but shifts toward a more commercial sound, enlisting Jermaine Dupri alongside Wu-affiliated producers True Master, Goldfinghaz, and Inspectah Deck behind the boards. Debuting at number 51, it marked a commercial decline from The Pillage but showcased Cap's versatility across boom-bap grit and R&B-inflected hooks. Ghostface, Raekwon, Killah Priest, and Da Brat all make appearances.

The Struggle

2003
Code:Red
Released independently on Code:Red Entertainment after parting ways with Epic, The Struggle is a sprawling 21-track affair that documents Cap's post-major-label hustle. With production from Calogero, Remedy, 4th Disciple, and Soulfingaz, the sound returns to a rawer, less polished aesthetic. Raekwon and Inspectah Deck contribute guest verses, while the extended runtime reflects an artist with something to prove on his own terms.

The Cappatilize Project

2008
Park Hill
Self-released on his own Park Hill Projects imprint, The Cappatilize Project marks Cappadonna's full embrace of independent distribution. Produced by Big Zick, Spinzilla, Solo, Q-Dini, and others, the fifteen tracks lean into motivational and spiritual themes alongside the street narratives. Born Divine appears on several cuts as Cap's primary collaborator for the era, replacing the major-label Clan roster with a tighter circle of Staten Island loyalists.

Slang Prostitution

2009
Chambermusik
Signed to the new Chambermusik imprint distributed through RED, Slang Prostitution finds Cappadonna reconnecting with the Wu family on wax — Masta Killa and Raekwon both appear — while building a new stable of collaborators including Lounge Lo, 3rd Digala, and JoJo Pellegrino. The nineteen tracks are produced by a wide bench of beatmakers including Soul Professor, Digem Tracks, and Blastah Beats, delivering a street-level New York sound that nods to the Clan's legacy without leaning on it.

The Pilgrimage

2011
Chamber Musik Fat Beats
Released through Chamber Musik, Fat Beats, and Soulspazm, The Pilgrimage strips back to a lean thirteen tracks and finds Cappadonna in a reflective, spiritually grounded mode. Inspectah Deck returns for two features, while newer collaborators Chedda Bang, Killa Black, and Matlock fill out the roster. At forty-five minutes, it's among his most focused post-Pillage efforts, balancing street narratives with Five Percenter wisdom.

Eyrth, Wynd & Fyre

2013
RBC
An ambitious double album released on RBC Records and E1 Music. Disc one, produced entirely by J. Glaze, delivers a cohesive, introspective fourteen-track suite where Cappadonna raps largely solo. Disc two — subtitled Love, Anger & Emotion — draws on Stu Bangas, Kevlaar 7, Keelay & Zaire, and DJ Snips for a more varied, collaborative sound with Lounge Mode and Sav Killz featuring throughout. At twenty-eight tracks, it's his most expansive statement.

Hook Off

2014
God Love
The first release on Cap's God Love Family Entertainment imprint, Hook Off is a seventeen-track project built around a revolving cast of collaborators including Amar Divine, Don Shae, Turnpike Ike, and Wonda Woman. PMD of EPMD and Spliff Star of Flipmode Squad make notable guest appearances, broadening the record beyond the usual Wu orbit. The title references Cap's ability to hold a track without melodic hooks — pure bars from start to finish.

Ear Candy

2018
God Love
Fifteen tracks released on God Love Family Entertainment that find Cappadonna in an introspective, eclectic mode. His son D Hill Jr. appears on two tracks, adding a family dimension to the project. The album ranges from boom-bap fundamentalism on cuts like "Licorice" to the contemplative "Soul Searching" and "Heaven," with Mike Noble and Killa Black contributing verses. The title signals Cap's intent to deliver pure lyrical pleasure — ear candy for the hip-hop purist.

Black Is Beautiful

2020
God Love
Released amid the racial justice movements of 2020, Black Is Beautiful is a twelve-track statement of Black pride and positivity. Big Nate All Star and PBS Skinz serve as near-constant collaborators across the project, with Desert Eagle, Carlton Fisk, Kennedy Price, and JoJo Pellegrino filling out the guest roster. The sound is smoother and more soulful than much of Cap's catalog, leaning into uplifting themes on tracks like "Sunshine" and "Issa Good Day."
