Veronica Falls

2011
Slumberland Bella Union
Their self-titled debut arrived with significant buzz from early singles on Captured Tracks. Produced by Guy Fixsen, it channels C86 jangle and Jesus and Mary Chain fuzz into twelve concise, hook-driven songs with a dark lyrical streak — graveyards, beachside cliffs, and a pervading sense of unease wrapped in irresistible melodies. The seamless interplay of four voices gives even the gloomiest moments an airy, communal warmth.

Waiting for Something to Happen

2013
💻 In library Slumberland Bella Union
The sophomore album pulls back from gothic imagery toward a more polished, emotionally direct sound. Produced by Rory Attwell, the guitars still fizz and jangle but harmonies are more refined and arrangements more confident, reflecting the transition from teenage restlessness into something more reflective. Songs like "Teenage" and "Shooting Star" balance wistfulness with propulsive energy. It turned out to be their final statement — the band dissolved quietly the following year.
