GZA/Genius

The Wu-Tang Clan's most cerebral sword — Gary Grice built a solo catalog on dense, interlocking wordplay, chess metaphors, and razor-precise delivery over RZA's stark, cinematic production. From the genre-defining Liquid Swords to his explorations of science and language, GZA treats every verse as a logic puzzle solved in real time.

Words from the Genius

1991
Cold Chillin'
The debut, released under the name The Genius before Wu-Tang Clan existed, on Cold Chillin' Records alongside Big Daddy Kane and Biz Markie. Produced primarily by Easy Mo Bee, the album showcases a young Gary Grice navigating late-golden-era production with an already distinctive verbal precision. It sank commercially due to poor label promotion, but tracks like the title cut and "Those Were the Days" hint at the lyrical rigor that would fully emerge once RZA rebuilt the sonic architecture around his cousin's voice.

Liquid Swords

1995
 🔈💻 Owned Geffen
The masterpiece. RZA's production reaches its iciest, most cinematic peak — stark piano loops, Shogun Assassin dialogue interludes, and cavernous drums frame GZA's most disciplined writing. Every verse on "4th Chamber," "Duel of the Iron Mic," and the title track operates with surgical precision, layering chess metaphors, street narratives, and martial-arts philosophy into a seamless whole. It cracked the Billboard Top Ten and remains one of the most acclaimed hip-hop albums ever recorded, the crown jewel of the Wu-Tang solo era.

Beneath the Surface

1999
 💻 In library MCA
The difficult follow-up to a classic. GZA diversified his production roster beyond RZA, enlisting Mathematics, Arabian Knight, and Inspectah Deck behind the boards, while executive-producing alongside the Abbott. The sound is warmer and more varied than Liquid Swords' permafrost, and tracks like "Amplified Sample" and "Breaker, Breaker" prove his pen remained sharp. It debuted at number one on the Hip-Hop/R&B chart, though critical consensus placed it a tier below its predecessor.

Legend of the Liquid Sword

2002
 💻 In library MCA
A spiritual sequel to his sophomore classic, drawing its title back into the Liquid Swords mythology. The production palette widens further — Bink!, DJ Muggs, Jaz-O, and Arabian Knight join RZA and Mathematics — and GZA sounds energized, particularly on "Auto Bio" (a vivid origin story tracing his path from Staten Island to Wu-Tang) and the Ghostface-featuring "Silent." Santi White (later Santigold) appears on "Stay in Line" years before her own debut, making this a quiet landmark of cross-genre collaboration.

Pro Tools

2008
Babygrande
The fifth and most recent studio album, released independently on Babygrande after a six-year gap. A. & R.'d by Dreddy Kruger, it reunites GZA with Wu-affiliated producers Bronze Nazareth, True Master, and Mathematics alongside newcomers Black Milk and Roc Marciano (who also raps on "Short Race"). The beats are leaner and more understated than the classic RZA sound, foregrounding GZA's pen above all else. "Pencil" and "Paper Plate" became underground favorites, and Ka's guest verse on "Firehouse" is a quiet landmark.
