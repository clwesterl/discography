Come On Pilgrim

1987
 🔈💻 Owned 4AD
Eight songs culled from the Purple Tape demo sessions at Fort Apache Studios, released as a mini-LP that announced something genuinely new. The bilingual lyrics, lurching dynamics, and Francis's feral vocal attack arrived fully formed, drawing equally from Husker Du's velocity and Buddy Holly's melodic instincts. Songs like "Caribou" and "Vamos" established the surrealist, reference-dense lyrical world the band would inhabit for the next four years.

Surfer Rosa

1988
 🔈💻 Owned 4AD
The debut full-length, produced by Steve Albini with his signature dry, explosive drum sound. The loud-quiet-loud template is fully weaponized here: "Where Is My Mind?" became the band's most enduring song, while Kim Deal's "Gigantic" proved she was a songwriting force in her own right. Albini's production gave every instrument a physical, confrontational presence that influenced a generation of alternative bands — Kurt Cobain famously cited it as the sonic blueprint for Nevermind.

Doolittle

1989
 💻 In library 4AD Elektra
The masterpiece and commercial breakthrough, produced by Gil Norton with a polish that sharpened rather than dulled the band's edges. From the surrealist assault of "Debaser" through the pop perfection of "Here Comes Your Man" to the environmental anxiety of "Monkey Gone to Heaven," all fifteen tracks sustain an extraordinary level of invention. It became a cornerstone of alternative rock, landing on virtually every best-of-the-decade list and selling over a million copies.

Bossanova

1990
 💻 In library 4AD Elektra
A sharp left turn into space rock, surf guitar, and science fiction. Gil Norton returned to produce, and Santiago's tremolo-bar explorations took center stage alongside Francis's obsession with UFOs, extraterrestrials, and the cosmos. "Velouria" and "Dig for Fire" became alt-rock radio staples, while deep cuts like "Havalina" and the instrumental opener "Cecilia Ann" (a cover of The Surftones) showed a band confidently expanding its palette. Kim Deal's role diminished here, fueling tensions that would eventually split the group.

Trompe le Monde

1991
4AD Elektra
The final album of the original era, and the heaviest — a relentless fifteen-track barrage that leans into thrash tempos and sci-fi themes. Francis dominates almost completely, with Deal's contributions reduced to near-zero as the interpersonal dysfunction peaked. "U-Mass," "Planet of Sound," and "Motorway to Roswell" are standouts, and the Jesus and Mary Chain cover "Head On" became a fan favorite. The band dissolved via fax shortly after touring in support of it, not to reconvene for thirteen years.

Indie Cindy

2014
Pixiesmusic
The comeback album, twenty-three years after Trompe le Monde and the first without Kim Deal, who departed during the sessions. Compiled from three EPs released incrementally, it reunited the band with Gil Norton at Rockfield Studios in Wales. Reviews were mixed — the songwriting doesn't match the original run's density — but "Bagboy" and "Blue Eyed Hexe" showed the band could still generate real menace and momentum. Kim Shattuck (Muffs) briefly filled in on bass before Paz Lenchantin joined permanently.

Head Carrier

2016
Pixiesmusic
The first album with Paz Lenchantin as a full member and the first produced by Tom Dalgety, who would helm the band's next three records. More cohesive than Indie Cindy, it foregrounds Lenchantin's harmonies and occasionally gives her the lead — "All I Think About Now" is an overt love letter to Kim Deal's "Gigantic." "Tenement Song" and "Baal's Back" recapture some of the classic era's twitchy energy, while the overall tone is darker and more deliberate.

Beneath the Eyrie

2019
BMG
Recorded at Dreamland Studios, a converted church in upstate New York, with the gothic setting seeping into the music. Tom Dalgety produced again, and the album leans into folk-tinged darkness, witchcraft imagery, and narrative storytelling. "Catfish Kate" and "On Graveyard Hill" build atmosphere over volume, while "Los Surfers Muertos" and "St. Nazaire" hint at broader melodic ambitions. An accompanying podcast documented the recording sessions, a first for the band.

Doggerel

2022
BMG
Recorded at Guilford Sound in Vermont, the eighth studio album finds the band in a looser, more expansive mode. Tom Dalgety produced once more, encouraging longer arrangements and more layered textures. "There's a Moon On" and "Vault of Heaven" push toward a spacious, almost classic-rock grandeur, while "Haunted House" and "Thunder and Lightning" retain the band's abrasive edge. Joey Santiago called it the grown-up version of Doolittle — a bold claim, but the ambition is audible.

The Night the Zombies Came

2024
BMG
The ninth studio album and fourth with Paz Lenchantin, produced once again by Tom Dalgety. A loose concept album with narrative threads about small-town America, zombies, and apocalyptic unease. "Chicken" and "You're So Impatient" recall the band's punchier side, while "The Vegas Suite" closes the record with an ambitious multi-part instrumental coda. Lenchantin's role continues to deepen, and the album was received as one of the stronger reunion-era entries.
