Bonnie “Prince” Billy

Will Oldham · Singer-songwriter, actor — Louisville, Kentucky

Will Oldham has spent three decades building one of American music’s most singular catalogs — a restless, shape-shifting body of work that moves between Appalachian folk, country, art-rock, and unclassifiable beauty. Recording under a shifting series of names (Palace Brothers, Palace Music, Palace, and finally Bonnie ‘Prince’ Billy), Oldham has made vulnerability, mortality, and the strangeness of being alive his enduring subjects.

In library Drag City
The masterpiece. Oldham’s most celebrated album is a meditation on friendship, death, and the thin membrane between joy and despair. Funtown Comedown

2009
Drag City with The Picket Line
Recorded live with Louisville bluegrass dance band The Picket Line. A joyous, communal set that reimagines Palace and BPB songs through a bluegrass lens. Includes a cover of Merle Haggard’s ‘Rambling Fever.’ Possibly the most fun Oldham record.
bluegrass live country

Superwolves

2021
In collection Drag City with Matt Sweeney
The long-awaited sequel to Superwolf, 16 years later. The duo’s chemistry is undiminished — Sweeney’s guitar is heavier and more textured, and the rhythm section (including Mdou Moctar and his band) adds a new dimension. ‘Hall of Death’ and ‘My Blue Suit’ are instant classics.
Personnel
Matt Sweeney (guitar), Mdou Moctar (lead guitar), Ahmoudou Madassane (rhythm guitar), Mike Coltun (bass), Souleyman Ibrahim (drums)
